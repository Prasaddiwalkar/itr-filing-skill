# ITR Filing Skill — Indian Salaried Employee Complete Guide

Covers every type of Indian salaried employee:
- Simple salary-only employee (ITR-1, 15-minute filing)
- Employee with stocks / mutual funds (ITR-2)
- Employee with home loan, HRA, multiple deductions
- Employee with foreign investments, RSUs, eTrade (ITR-2 complex)

---

## PHASE 1: Understand the Employee's Profile

Start with these questions to determine the filing path:

### Quick Profile Questions
1. Single employer or changed jobs during the year?
2. Approximate total income (to determine ITR form)?
3. Living in rented house or own house?
4. Any stocks, mutual funds, or FDs?
5. Any foreign investments (eTrade, Vested, foreign bank account)?

### Determine Filing Path

Based on answers, the employee falls into one of three paths:

---

### 🟢 PATH A — Simple Salaried (ITR-1)
**Qualifies if ALL of these are true:**
- Only salary income (single or multiple employers)
- Income ≤ ₹50 lakhs
- No capital gains (no stocks sold, no MF redeemed)
- No foreign assets or foreign income
- Maximum one house property
- Not a company director
- No VDA/crypto
- Agricultural income ≤ ₹5,000

**→ Go to: Simple ITR-1 Filing Guide (Section A below)**

---

### 🟡 PATH B — Domestic Investor (ITR-2, No Foreign)
**Qualifies if:**
- Salary + capital gains from Indian stocks/MF
- OR salary > ₹50 lakhs
- OR home loan with let-out property
- OR director in Indian company
- But NO foreign assets/income

**→ Go to: ITR-2 Domestic Guide (Section B below)**

> ⚠️ If total income > ₹50 lakhs: **also read Section D** for mandatory
> Schedule AL (assets & liabilities) and surcharge computation.

---

### 🔴 PATH C — Foreign Investor / RSU Holder (ITR-2, Complex)
**Qualifies if:**
- Holds eTrade / Vested / foreign brokerage account
- OR received RSU/ESOP from foreign employer
- OR has any foreign bank account
- OR has any other foreign income

**→ Go to: ITR-2 Foreign Guide (Section C below)**

> ⚠️ If total income > ₹50 lakhs: **also read Section D** for mandatory
> Schedule AL (assets & liabilities) and surcharge computation.

## SECTION A: Simple Salaried Employee — ITR-1 Guide

### Documents Needed (Minimum)
- [ ] **Form 16 Part A + B** — from employer (issued by 15 June)
- [ ] **Form 26AS** — download from IT portal for current AY
- [ ] **AIS / TIS** — Annual Information Statement from IT portal
- [ ] **Bank statements** — all savings accounts (for interest income)
- [ ] **FD interest certificates** (if any fixed deposits)

**If claiming deductions (Old Regime only):**
- [ ] Rent receipts + landlord PAN (HRA)
- [ ] Home loan interest certificate (Section 24b)
- [ ] LIC/PPF/ELSS receipts (80C)
- [ ] Health insurance premium receipts (80D)

### ITR-1 Quick Filing — Step by Step

**Login** → incometax.gov.in → e-File → Income Tax Returns → File ITR

#### Step 1: Start
- AY: 2026-27 (for FY 2025-26)
- Mode: Online | Type: Original Return | Form: **ITR-1**

#### Step 2: General Information
- Verify pre-filled: PAN, Name, DOB, Aadhaar, Address
- Update mobile/email if changed
- **Tax Regime**: New (115BAC) or Old — select carefully
- Add bank account for refund

#### Step 3: Salary (Pre-filled — Just Verify)
Portal pre-fills from Form 16. Check these match your Form 16 Part B:
- Gross Salary = 17(1) + 17(2) + 17(3)
- HRA exemption u/s 10(13A) — may need manual correction
- Standard Deduction = ₹75,000 (auto-applied)
- Net Taxable Salary

> If pre-filled salary is wrong, click Edit and enter from Form 16 directly.

#### Step 4: House Property
- Self-occupied with home loan (Old Regime): enter interest paid (max ₹2,00,000)
- No property / renting: leave as ₹0 / skip

#### Step 5: Other Sources
Portal pre-fills from AIS. Verify:
- Savings account interest (all banks combined)
- FD interest (if any)
- Dividend income (if any Indian stocks held)

> Accept pre-filled figures if higher than your calculation. Never reduce below AIS.

#### Step 6: Deductions (Old Regime only)
If Old Regime selected, fill Schedule VI-A:
- 80C: PPF + ELSS + LIC + EPF employee share + home loan principal (max ₹1.5L combined)
- 80CCD(1B): NPS additional self-contribution (max ₹50,000 extra)
- 80D: Health insurance premiums — self (₹25,000) + parents (₹25,000/₹50,000)
- 80TTA: Savings bank interest deduction (max ₹10,000)

#### Step 7: Tax Summary
- Review pre-computed tax
- Check Rebate u/s 87A is applied (if eligible)
- If tax due → pay Self Assessment Tax (e-Pay Tax → code 300)
- If refund → will be credited to selected bank account

#### Step 8: Submit and Verify
- Preview → Submit → e-Verify via Aadhaar OTP (fastest)
- Download ITR-V acknowledgement

**That's it! For a simple salary-only employee, this takes 15-30 minutes.**

## SECTION B: Domestic Investor — ITR-2 Guide (No Foreign Investments)

### Documents Needed
**Core (same as ITR-1):**
- [ ] Form 16 Part A + B
- [ ] Form 26AS (AY specific)
- [ ] AIS / TIS

**For capital gains:**
- [ ] Zerodha / Groww / broker Tax P&L report (FY specific)
- [ ] Tradewise exit report (for scrip-wise Schedule 112A entry)
- [ ] Consolidated MF Tax Statement (CAMS or KFintech — camsonline.com)

**For home loan:**
- [ ] Interest certificate from bank (Section 24b)
- [ ] Principal certificate (80C — Old Regime)

**For other income:**
- [ ] All bank statements (savings interest)
- [ ] FD interest certificates

**For deductions (Old Regime):**
- [ ] Insurance, PPF, NPS receipts (80C/80D/80CCD)

### Select Schedules (ITR-2, No Foreign)
| Schedule | Select When |
|---|---|
| Part A-Gen | Always |
| Schedule S | Always — salary |
| Schedule HP | Home loan or let-out property |
| Schedule OS | Dividends, interest, any other income |
| Schedule CG | Any stock/MF capital gains or losses |
| Schedule CYLA | Current year losses to set off |
| Schedule BFLA | Prior year losses being set off |
| Schedule CFL | Losses being carried forward |
| Schedule VI-A | Old Regime deductions (80C, 80D etc) |
| Schedule 80G | Donations |
| Schedule AL | Mandatory if total income > ₹50 lakhs |
| Schedule AL | **Mandatory if total income > ₹50 lakhs** — assets & liabilities |
| Schedule VDA | Crypto/virtual digital assets |

> Note: Schedule FA, FSI, TR — **NOT needed** if no foreign investments.
> Note: Schedule AL — **mandatory** if income > ₹50L regardless of foreign/domestic status.
> See Section D above for complete Schedule AL and surcharge guidance.

### Capital Gains — Key Points

#### STCG (Equity held ≤ 12 months): 20% tax
#### LTCG (Equity held > 12 months): 12.5% above ₹1.25L exemption

#### Section 112A — Listed Equity LTCG Entry
- In Schedule CG → B1(a) → "View Schedule 112A"
- Enter scrip-wise: ISIN, name, units, sale value, purchase cost
- FMV on 31 Jan 2018 = ₹0 for stocks bought after that date
- Portal auto-calculates ₹1.25L exemption and tax

#### MF Capital Gains
- Equity MF (held > 12 months): LTCG at 12.5%
- Equity MF (held ≤ 12 months): STCG at 20%
- Debt MF (bought after Apr 2023): taxed at slab rate
- Get breakup from CAMS/KFintech consolidated tax statement

#### Loss Carry Forward
- STCG and LTCG losses can be carried forward for **8 years**
- House property loss can be set off against salary (max ₹2L/year)
- **Must file before 31 July** to carry forward any losses

## SECTION C: Foreign Investor — ITR-2 Complex Guide

*Only for employees with: eTrade / Vested / foreign brokerage / RSU/ESOP /
foreign bank accounts / any foreign income*

### Additional Documents Needed (over Section B)
- [ ] Foreign broker annual statement (eTrade, Vested, Interactive Brokers etc)
- [ ] Form 1042-S (US — shows dividend income + withholding)
- [ ] RBI FBIL reference rates file (rbi.org.in → FBIL → Archive → download XLS)
- [ ] Peak portfolio value + 31-Dec closing balance (from broker statements)

### Additional Schedules Needed
| Schedule | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Schedule FA | Declare foreign brokerage account / assets |
| Schedule FSI | Report foreign dividend / income in INR |
| Schedule TR | Claim DTAA tax relief |
| Schedule AL | **Mandatory if total income > ₹50 lakhs** |

> ⚠️ FA + FSI + TR mandatory for ALL foreign asset holders.
> ⚠️ AL mandatory if total income > ₹50L — see Section D for full guidance.
> Penalty for non-disclosure = ₹10 lakh minimum (Black Money Act 2015).
> Declare even if foreign account balance is zero at year end.

### RSU / ESOP from Foreign Employer (e.g. Equinix, Google, Microsoft)
| RSU Event | Tax Treatment |
|---|---|
| Vesting | Perquisite u/s 17(2) — already in Form 16 salary |
| Dividend on vested RSUs | Foreign dividend → Schedule FSI + Schedule OS |
| Sale of vested RSUs | Capital gains → Schedule CG |

> Cost of acquisition for RSU sale = FMV on vest date (not ₹0 — it was already taxed as perquisite)

> Schedule ESOP on portal = only for eligible **Indian startups** — MNC employees click "Back to Summary"

### Schedule FA — Foreign Assets
Use **Section A2** for brokerage accounts (eTrade, Vested):
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Country | United States (US) |
| Institution | E*TRADE from Morgan Stanley |
| Address | 1 New York Plaza, 7th Floor, New York, NY 10004 |
| Account number | From statement cover page |
| Peak balance during year | Highest portfolio value Jan–Dec (INR) |
| Closing balance | Portfolio value on 31 Dec (INR) |
| Income | Total dividends received Jan–Dec (INR) |

> FA uses **calendar year (1 Jan – 31 Dec)** — NOT Indian FY (Apr–Mar)

### Schedule FSI — Foreign Source Income
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Country code | US |
| Head of income | Other Sources (for dividends) |
| Income from outside India | Foreign dividend in INR |
| Tax paid outside India | US withholding tax in INR |
| DTAA article | Article 10 (India-USA dividends) |

> Foreign dividends in Schedule FSI must ALSO be manually added to Schedule OS 1(a)(i).
> FSI alone does NOT auto-populate total income computation.

### Schedule TR — DTAA Tax Relief
- Section 90 for DTAA countries (USA, UK, Singapore etc)
- Relief = **lesser of** (foreign tax paid) or (Indian tax on that income)
- India-USA DTAA caps dividend relief at **15%** (US typically withholds 25%)
- Add via "+ Add More Schedules" if not visible
- See `references/dtaa-rates.md` for all country rates

### USD to INR Conversion (RBI Rates)
- Use quarter-end rates from RBI FBIL archive:
  - Q1 end: 30 June rate
  - Q2 end: 30 September rate
  - Q3 end: 31 December rate ← use this for FA closing balance
  - Q4 end: 31 March rate

## SECTION D: High Income Employee — Income > ₹50 Lakhs

*This section applies to Path B and Path C employees whose total income exceeds ₹50 lakhs.*

### Two Extra Mandatory Requirements

When total income (salary + all other sources) exceeds **₹50 lakhs**:

1. **Schedule AL** — Must declare all assets and liabilities as of 31 March
2. **Surcharge** — Additional tax over and above basic tax + cess

---

### Schedule AL — Assets and Liabilities Declaration

**Who must file:** Every taxpayer whose total income > ₹50 lakhs.
**As of date:** 31 March of the financial year (e.g. 31 March 2026 for AY 2026-27).
**Consequence of not filing:** Defective return notice u/s 139(9).

#### What to Declare

| Category | What to Include | Value to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Immovable Property** | All land, plots, residential/commercial buildings | Cost of acquisition (purchase price + stamp duty + registration) |
| **Jewellery and bullion** | Gold jewellery, silver, diamonds, precious stones | Purchase cost |
| **Archaeological collections / paintings / sculptures** | Art, antiques | Purchase cost |
| **Vehicles** | Cars, two-wheelers, boats, aircraft | Purchase cost |
| **Financial assets — shares/securities** | All demat holdings (Indian + foreign stocks, MF units, bonds, debentures) | Cost of acquisition |
| **Financial assets — bank deposits** | All savings accounts, FDs, RDs across all banks | Balance as on 31 March |
| **Financial assets — life insurance** | LIC / ULIP policies — surrender value | Surrender/maturity value |
| **Financial assets — loans** | Money lent to others | Outstanding amount |
| **Cash in hand** | Physical cash held | Actual amount |
| **Any other asset** | PPF balance, NPS corpus, EPF balance, any other asset | Current value |

#### Liabilities to Declare

| Liability | What to Include |
|---|---|
| Home loan | Outstanding principal as on 31 March |
| Car loan | Outstanding principal as on 31 March |
| Personal loan | Outstanding principal |
| Education loan | Outstanding principal |
| Credit card dues | Outstanding balance |
| Any other borrowings | Outstanding amount |

#### Common Mistakes in Schedule AL

| Mistake | Correct Approach |
|---|---|
| Showing market value of property | Use **cost of acquisition** (purchase price), not current market value |
| Forgetting inherited property | Must declare even if inherited — use market value at time of inheritance as cost |
| Missing spouse's jointly held property | Declare your share / proportionate ownership |
| Forgetting old FDs | Include ALL bank accounts and FDs across all banks |
| Not declaring EPF / PPF | These are assets — include EPF corpus and PPF balance |
| Ignoring foreign assets | Foreign assets go in BOTH Schedule FA and Schedule AL |
| Leaving vehicle out | All vehicles — even two-wheelers |

#### Documents Needed for Schedule AL
- Property purchase agreements (for cost)
- Vehicle registration certificate (for purchase price)
- Bank statements as of 31 March (for balances)
- Demat account statement as of 31 March (for holdings + cost)
- Home loan / car loan outstanding statement as of 31 March
- EPF passbook / NPS statement (for corpus value)

---

### Surcharge — Additional Tax for High Earners

Surcharge is levied on the **income tax amount** (before cess) when income exceeds ₹50 lakhs.

#### Surcharge Rates

| Total Income | Surcharge Rate | Effective Rate (approx) |
|---|---|---|
| Up to ₹50 lakhs | **Nil** | — |
| ₹50L – ₹1 Crore | **10%** | 33% (30% tax + 10% surcharge) |
| ₹1Cr – ₹2 Crore | **15%** | 34.5% |
| ₹2Cr – ₹5 Crore | **25%** | 37.5% |
| Above ₹5 Crore | **25%** (New Regime) / **37%** (Old Regime) | Up to 42.74% |

> Note: For LTCG (112A) and STCG income, surcharge is capped at **15%** regardless
> of total income. This is a significant benefit for high-income investors.

#### Health and Education Cess

**4%** on (income tax + surcharge) — applies to everyone, no threshold.

#### Marginal Relief

When income slightly exceeds ₹50L / ₹1Cr thresholds, the extra tax (surcharge)
should not exceed the incremental income. Portal applies marginal relief automatically.
No action needed — verify it appears in Part B-TTI.

#### Example Tax Computation (Income = ₹75 Lakhs, New Regime, AY 2026-27)

| Step | Calculation | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Total Income | — | ₹75,00,000 |
| Less Standard Deduction | — | -₹75,000 |
| Taxable Income | — | ₹74,25,000 |
| Tax on ₹74.25L (as per slabs) | — | ₹16,87,500 |
| Surcharge @ 10% (income > ₹50L) | 10% × ₹16,87,500 | ₹1,68,750 |
| Tax + Surcharge | — | ₹18,56,250 |
| Health & Education Cess @ 4% | 4% × ₹18,56,250 | ₹74,250 |
| **Total Tax Liability** | — | **₹19,30,500** |

---

### Part B-TTI — Final Tax Computation Verification

For high-income employees, verify each line carefully:

| Line | Description | What to Check |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Total income | Matches sum of all schedules |
| 2 | Tax at normal slab rates | Correct new/old regime slab applied |
| 3 | Tax at special rates (STCG/LTCG/VDA) | 20% STCG, 12.5% LTCG, 30% VDA |
| 5 | **Surcharge** | Correct rate applied per slab above |
| 6 | **Health & Education Cess @ 4%** | On tax + surcharge |
| 7 | Total tax liability | Sum of lines 2+3+5+6 |
| 10 | **Marginal relief** | Portal applies automatically if applicable |
| 11 | Relief u/s 89 | Salary arrears relief (Form 10E) |
| 12 | Relief u/s 90/90A | DTAA credit (Schedule TR) |
| 15 | Total taxes paid | TDS + TCS + advance tax + SAT |
| 16 | **Net tax payable / refundable** | Should be ₹0 or refund |

> ⚠️ High-income employees often have balance tax due because:
> - Employer TDS calculated on salary only — does not account for FD interest,
>   dividend, capital gains, or foreign income
> - Surcharge not accounted for in employer TDS if salary alone is near ₹50L
>   threshold but other income pushes it over
> - Always pay Self Assessment Tax for any shortfall BEFORE submitting ITR

### Advance Tax Obligation (Income > ₹10,000 tax after TDS)

If total tax liability after TDS credits exceeds ₹10,000, advance tax
must have been paid in quarterly instalments during the year:

| Instalment | Due Date | Cumulative % |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 | 15 June | 15% |
| Q2 | 15 September | 45% |
| Q3 | 15 December | 75% |
| Q4 | 15 March | 100% |

If advance tax was not paid / underpaid → interest u/s 234B and 234C applies.
Compute and pay along with SAT before filing.

## COMMON SALARY TOPICS

### Form 16 — What to Verify
| Check | Where |
|---|---|
| Gross salary correct | Part B Row 1(d) |
| HRA exemption calculated correctly | Part B Row 2(e) |
| Standard deduction = ₹75,000 | Part B Row 4(a) |
| TDS total matches Form 26AS | Part A total = 26AS Section 192 |
| Tax regime correct (New/Old) | Part B Row A |

### HRA Exemption — The 3-Rule Minimum
Exempt HRA = Minimum of:
1. Actual HRA received from employer
2. Rent paid minus 10% of (Basic + DA)
3. 50% of salary (Delhi/Mumbai/Chennai/Kolkata) OR 40% (all other cities)

**Documents needed**: Monthly rent receipts, rent agreement, landlord PAN
(mandatory if annual rent > ₹1 lakh)

**WFH + HRA**: Still claimable if actually paying rent — need genuine
rent agreement and bank transfer proof.

**Paying rent to parents**: Valid — maintain rent agreement + bank transfers.

### Salary Arrears — Section 89 Relief
If received arrears (previous years' salary in current year):
1. File **Form 10E** on IT portal FIRST (before submitting ITR)
2. Enter relief amount in Schedule S Row 18
3. Attach Form 10E acknowledgement reference

> ⚠️ Form 10E must be filed BEFORE ITR — not after.

### Multiple Employers (Job Change)
- Collect Form 16 from both employers
- Declare salary from both in Schedule S separately
- Add TDS from both in Tax Paid schedule
- Combined salary may create shortfall in TDS — pay Self Assessment Tax

### Bonus
- Included in Form 16 as normal salary — no separate declaration needed
- TDS should have been deducted by employer at time of payment

### EPF Basics
| Situation | Tax Treatment |
|---|---|
| Normal EPF (contribution ≤ ₹2.5L/year) | Interest: exempt. Maturity (5+ yr service): exempt |
| Excess EPF (contribution > ₹2.5L/year) | Interest on excess: taxable — report in OS |
| Withdrawal before 5 years | Taxable as salary. TDS @ 10% (20% without PAN) |
| EPF transfer on job change | Not taxable — just a transfer |

### Gratuity
| Category | Exemption |
|---|---|
| Government employees | Fully exempt |
| Private (Gratuity Act covered) | Min of: actual, ₹20L, or 15/26 × salary × years |
| Private (not covered) | Min of: actual, ₹10L, or ½ × salary × years |

## REGIME SELECTION

### New Regime (Default from FY 2024-25)
- Standard deduction ₹75,000 included
- Rebate u/s 87A: up to ₹60,000 if income ≤ ₹12L (effectively zero tax up to ₹12.75L)
- No 80C, 80D, HRA, LTA, home loan deductions
- Simpler calculation

### Old Regime
- All deductions available (80C, 80D, HRA, LTA, Section 24b etc)
- Rebate u/s 87A: up to ₹12,500 if income ≤ ₹5L
- Usually better only if total deductions exceed ~₹3.5-4L

### Surcharge on High Income
Applies over and above basic tax when income > ₹50L:
- ₹50L – ₹1Cr: **+10%** on tax
- ₹1Cr – ₹2Cr: **+15%** on tax
- ₹2Cr – ₹5Cr: **+25%** on tax
- Above ₹5Cr: **+25%** (New) / **+37%** (Old)

**Health & Education Cess: 4%** on (tax + surcharge) — applies to everyone.

> LTCG/STCG surcharge capped at 15% regardless of total income.
> See Section D for complete surcharge and Schedule AL guidance.

### Switching Regime
- New → Old: File **Form 10-IEA** on portal BEFORE/while filing ITR
- Old → New: Just select New Regime in ITR (no form needed)
- Salaried employees can switch every year
- What employer deducted during year does NOT bind your ITR filing choice

See `references/regime-guide.md` for detailed comparison and break-even analysis.

## TAX PAID AND REFUND

### Sources of Tax Credit
| Source | Section | Where to Find |
|---|---|---|
| TDS on salary | 192 | Form 16 / Form 26AS |
| TDS on interest / FD | 194A | Form 26AS / AIS |
| TDS on dividend | 194 | Form 26AS |
| TDS on MF income | 194K | Form 26AS |
| TCS on LRS remittances | 206CQ | Form 26AS / AIS |
| TCS on car purchase > ₹10L | 206C | Form 26AS |
| Advance tax paid | — | Challan receipts |

> Check AIS for TCS-206CQ entries if you did foreign remittances via LRS — often missed.

### Self Assessment Tax (If Tax Due)
1. incometax.gov.in → **e-Pay Tax** → SAT (Minor head 300)
2. Pay via net banking / UPI
3. Note **BSR Code** (7 digits) + **Challan Serial Number** (5 digits)
4. In ITR → Tax Paid → Add challan details
5. Part B-TTI should then show ₹0 due

### Rebate u/s 87A
- New Regime: up to ₹60,000 rebate if total income ≤ ₹12,00,000
- Old Regime: up to ₹12,500 rebate if total income ≤ ₹5,00,000
- Does NOT apply to STCG, LTCG, or VDA income (special rate income)

| Error | Fix |
|---|---|
| "Description mandatory" in 17(2)/(3) | Enter `RSU Perquisite` or `Nil` (no special chars) |
| "Invalid input" in description | Remove `( ) / - * #` from text |
| Pre-filled salary wrong | Click Edit → enter from Form 16 manually |
| HRA exemption not applied | Enter manually in Schedule S exemptions |
| Schedule CFL shows ₹0 despite CG loss | Save Schedule CG first, then open CFL |
| FSI income missing from total | Add manually to Schedule OS 1(a)(i) |
| CSV upload error `csv_row_skip` | Column 1 must say exactly: `after 31st Jan 2018` |
| Schedule FA/FSI/TR not in list | Click "+ Add More Schedules" |
| Tax relief showing ₹0 | Complete and confirm Schedule TR fully |
| ESOP mandatory fields (MNC employee) | Click "Back to Summary" — not applicable |
| Balance tax not reducing after SAT | Add challan in Tax Paid schedule |
| 87A rebate not applied | Special rate income (CG/VDA) excluded from rebate |
| Defective return notice 139(9) | Respond within 15 days on portal |
| AIS mismatch notice | File revised return with correct figures |

## SPECIAL SITUATIONS

### Retired / Pensioners
- Pension from employer: Schedule S — salary income
- Family pension: Schedule OS — 1/3rd or ₹15,000 exempt (whichever lower)
- Senior citizens: higher 80D limit (₹50K), 80TTB instead of 80TTA

### Job Change (Partial Year Employment)
- Collect Form 16 from both employers
- Combined salary may undershoot TDS → pay SAT before filing
- Submit Form 12B to new employer declaring previous salary

### Contract / Consultant (TDS u/s 194J — not 192)
- Receives Form 16A not Form 16
- No standard deduction applicable
- Use ITR-3 or ITR-4, not ITR-1/2

### NRI (Non-Resident Indian)
- Use ITR-2 with residential status: Non-Resident
- Only Indian-sourced income taxable in India
- NRE account interest: exempt. NRO account interest: taxable

### Deceased (Legal Heir Filing)
- Register as legal heir on IT portal first
- Report income only till date of death
- Inheritance received is NOT income

## VERIFICATION AND SUBMISSION

### E-Verification Methods
| Method | Speed | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Aadhaar OTP** | Instant | Most people — Aadhaar linked to mobile |
| Net banking EVC | Instant | If Aadhaar OTP fails |
| Demat account EVC | Instant | Through broker portal |
| Physical ITR-V | Up to 30 days | Print, sign, post to CPC Bengaluru |

> E-verify within **30 days** of filing. Unverified = not filed.

### Pre-Submission Checklist
- [ ] All income declared (salary + interest + dividends + capital gains)
- [ ] All deductions claimed (80C/80D/HRA if Old Regime)
- [ ] **Schedule AL filled if income > ₹50 lakhs** (assets + liabilities as of 31 March)
- [ ] **Surcharge correctly applied** in Part B-TTI (if income > ₹50L)
- [ ] **Cess @ 4%** applied on tax + surcharge in Part B-TTI
- [ ] Foreign schedules filled (FA/FSI/TR — if applicable)
- [ ] Advance tax shortfall + interest (234B/234C) paid via SAT
- [ ] SAT challan added in Tax Paid schedule
- [ ] Part B-TTI Line 16 shows ₹0 due or refund
- [ ] No red validation errors

## POST-FILING

### After Submitting
- Download and save **ITR-V Acknowledgement** (emailed + on portal)
- Save the **15-digit Acknowledgement Number**
- Check processing: portal → e-File → View Filed Returns

### Refund
- **Intimation u/s 143(1)**: issued within 15–90 days
- "No demand no refund" = correctly filed
- Refund: credited within 7–30 days after 143(1)
- If refund not received: check bank pre-validation on portal

### If Notice Received
| Notice | Action |
|---|---|
| 139(9) Defective return | Respond within 15 days on portal |
| 143(1) Demand | Verify and pay or dispute within 30 days |
| 148 Escaped income | Consult CA |

### Revised Return
- File before **31 December** of AY to correct any mistakes
- Select "Revised Return" → provide original acknowledgement number
