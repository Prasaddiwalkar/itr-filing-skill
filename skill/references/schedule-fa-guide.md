# Schedule FA — Foreign Assets Declaration Complete Guide

## Who Must File Schedule FA
ANY resident Indian (ROR status) who:
- Held any foreign asset at ANY time during the calendar year (1 Jan – 31 Dec)
- Had signing authority in any foreign account
- Had beneficial ownership of foreign assets

This includes NRIs who returned to India and became resident during the year.

## Penalty for Non-Disclosure
- Minimum ₹10,00,000 per undisclosed foreign asset (Black Money Act 2015)
- Criminal prosecution possible for willful evasion
- No time limit for prosecution
- Even zero-balance accounts must be declared if held during the year

## Reporting Period
**Calendar year: 1 January to 31 December** (NOT Indian FY Apr-Mar)

Example: For ITR filed for FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27),
Schedule FA covers calendar year **1 Jan 2025 to 31 Dec 2025**

## Asset Categories

### A1 — Foreign Depository Account (Bank Account)
- Savings, current, NRE, NRO accounts outside India
- Fields: Bank name, address, IBAN/account number, peak balance, closing balance, interest earned

### A2 — Foreign Custodial Account (Brokerage Account)
- Stock brokerage accounts holding securities (eTrade, Interactive Brokers, Vested, Schwab)
- Fields: Institution name, address, account number, peak value, 31-Dec value, income (dividends)

### A3 — Foreign Equity and Debt Interest (Direct Shareholding)
- Direct ownership in a foreign company (not through broker)
- Fields: Company name, country, nature of interest (equity/debt), acquisition date, cost, peak value

### A4 — Foreign Immovable Property
- Land, building, apartment outside India
- Fields: Address, nature (residential/commercial), acquisition date, cost, if sold during year

### A5 — Foreign Asset With Signing Authority
- Account where you have signing authority but are not the owner
- Example: Company's foreign bank account where you are authorized signatory

### A6 — Foreign Trust
- Trust created or settled outside India where you are trustee/beneficiary/settler

### A7 — Any Other Foreign Asset
- Foreign gold, jewelry, art, intellectual property, cryptocurrency held on foreign exchange
- Pension accounts like 401k, RRSP, CPF (not covered by 89A exemption for reporting)

## Filling for eTrade (Most Common)

**Use Section A2 — Foreign Custodial Account**

| Field | How to Get It |
|---|---|
| Country | United States |
| Name of institution | E*TRADE from Morgan Stanley |
| Address | 1 New York Plaza, 7th Floor, New York, NY 10004, USA |
| ZIP/Postal code | 10004 |
| Account number | Cover page of annual statement |
| Date of opening | When eTrade account was first opened |
| Status of account | Open |
| Whether owner or beneficial owner | Owner |
| Peak balance during year | Highest total account value Jan-Dec from monthly statements |
| Closing balance (31 Dec) | Portfolio value on 31 December from statement |
| Gross amount paid/credited (income) | Total dividends received during Jan-Dec in INR |

## INR Conversion
- Use RBI FBIL reference rate
- For closing balance (31 Dec): use 31 Dec rate
- For peak balance: use rate on date of peak value
- For income: use rate on date of receipt, or average rate
- FY 2025-26 approximate: $1 = ₹87-94 (varied through year)

## For Employer-Granted RSUs / ESOPs (Unvested)
- Do NOT declare unvested RSUs in Schedule FA
- Only declare **vested** RSUs that you actually own and hold in eTrade
- Unvested = contingent, not yet an asset

## Multiple Foreign Accounts
Add each account separately. Common for:
- eTrade + Interactive Brokers
- NRE bank account held before returning to India
- Joint account with foreign spouse
