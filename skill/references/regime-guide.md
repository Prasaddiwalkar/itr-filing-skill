# New Regime vs Old Regime — Complete Comparison Guide

## At a Glance

| Feature | New Regime | Old Regime |
|---|---|---|
| Default from FY 2024-25 | YES — default | Must opt in explicitly |
| Standard deduction | ₹75,000 | ₹50,000 |
| HRA exemption | ❌ Not available | ✅ Available |
| LTA exemption | ❌ Not available | ✅ Available |
| Home loan interest (self-occ) | ❌ Not available | ✅ Up to ₹2L |
| 80C (LIC/PPF/ELSS/EPF) | ❌ Not available | ✅ Up to ₹1.5L |
| 80D (health insurance) | ❌ Not available | ✅ Up to ₹1L |
| 80CCD(1B) NPS self | ❌ Not available | ✅ ₹50,000 extra |
| 80CCD(2) Employer NPS | ✅ Available | ✅ Available |
| Rebate u/s 87A | ₹60,000 (income ≤ ₹12L) | ₹12,500 (income ≤ ₹5L) |
| Complexity | Simple — fewer calculations | Complex — many deductions |

## When to Choose Old Regime

Use this rough guide:

| Situation | Likely Better Regime |
|---|---|
| Salary < ₹7L, no investments | New Regime (zero tax after rebate) |
| Salary ₹7L – ₹15L, few deductions | New Regime |
| Salary ₹7L – ₹15L, 80C + HRA + home loan large | Old Regime |
| Salary > ₹15L, deductions > ₹4L | Old Regime (check both) |
| Senior citizen with heavy medical expenses | Old Regime |

## Break-Even Analysis (Approximate)
Old Regime saves tax when total deductions exceed roughly:
- Income ₹10L: Need deductions > ₹2.5L to benefit from Old Regime
- Income ₹15L: Need deductions > ₹3.5L
- Income ₹20L: Need deductions > ₹4.5L
Always compute both and compare.

## Switching Between Regimes

### Salaried Employees (No Business Income)
- Can switch every financial year
- New → Old: File **Form 10-IEA** on IT portal BEFORE/while filing ITR
- Old → New: Simply select New Regime in ITR (no form needed)
- Employer's TDS during year doesn't bind your ITR filing choice

### Steps to Switch to Old Regime
1. Go to incometax.gov.in → e-File → Income Tax Forms → Form 10-IEA
2. Fill and submit Form 10-IEA for the assessment year
3. Note acknowledgement number
4. In ITR: select "Old Regime" and mention Form 10-IEA reference
5. Claim all eligible deductions in Schedule VI-A

### Business Income Holders
- Once you opt out of New Regime → Old Regime, you can only switch back once
- Cannot switch repeatedly like salaried employees

## Deductions Available in New Regime
Only these are allowed in New Regime:
1. Standard deduction ₹75,000 (Section 16ia)
2. Professional tax (Section 16iii)
3. Employer NPS contribution u/s 80CCD(2) — up to 14% of salary
4. Home loan interest on let-out property u/s 24 (not self-occupied)
5. DTAA tax relief (Schedule TR)
6. TCS credits

## Common Mistakes
- Claiming 80C/HRA in New Regime → Rejected by portal
- Not filing Form 10-IEA before claiming Old Regime deductions
- Comparing regimes on gross salary without applying deductions
- Not considering 87A rebate when computing New Regime tax
