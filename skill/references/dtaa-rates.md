# DTAA — Double Tax Avoidance Agreement Reference

## Key Concept
DTAA prevents the same income from being taxed twice — once in India and
once in the foreign country. India has DTAA with 90+ countries.

## How to Claim Relief
1. Declare foreign income in **Schedule FSI**
2. Declare foreign tax paid in Schedule FSI
3. Claim relief in **Schedule TR** under Section 90 (DTAA) or 91 (non-DTAA)
4. Relief = **lesser of** (a) tax paid abroad, (b) Indian tax on that income
5. DTAA-specified rate may further cap the relief

## Dividend Tax Rates Under DTAA

| Country | DTAA Dividend Rate | Section |
|---|---|---|
| USA | 15% (≥10% holding) / 25% (others) | Article 10 |
| UK | 15% | Article 10 |
| Singapore | 10% (≥25% holding) / 15% (others) | Article 10 |
| UAE | No withholding on dividends | — |
| Germany | 10% (≥10% holding) / 10% | Article 10 |
| Canada | 15% (≥10% holding) / 25% (others) | Article 10 |
| Australia | 15% (≥10% holding) / 15% | Article 10 |
| Netherlands | 10% (≥10% holding) / 10% | Article 10 |
| France | 10% | Article 10 |
| Japan | 10% (≥25% holding) / 10% | Article 10 |

## Non-DTAA Countries
Use Section 91 instead of 90. Relief = Indian tax rate on that income
(not the foreign tax rate). Less beneficial than DTAA countries.

## For US Investors (Most Common)

### Typical Scenario
- eTrade account holds US stocks (Apple, Google, Microsoft etc)
- US withholds **25%** on dividends from Indian residents
- India-USA DTAA Article 10 caps rate at **15%**
- India gives credit of 15% only — excess 10% is lost

### Calculation Example
- Gross US dividend: $1,423
- US withheld @ 25%: $356
- DTAA rate: 15%
- Credit allowed: 15% × $1,423 = $213.45
- Lost to excess withholding: $142.55

### How to Recover Excess US Withholding
File Form 1040-NR with US IRS to claim refund of excess withholding.
Or submit W-8BEN to broker specifying India-USA DTAA rate (15%) before
dividends are paid — future withholding will be at 15% only.

## RBI Reference Rates for Conversion

Source: rbi.org.in → Financial Markets → FBIL → Reference Rate Archive

Download as XLS/HTML for full year. Key quarter-end dates:
- 30 June (Q1 end)
- 30 September (Q2 end)
- 31 December (Q3 end / Calendar year end — important for Schedule FA)
- 31 March (Q4 end / FY end)

Use the rate on the date income was received for precise conversion.
For annual summary declarations, year-average rate is acceptable.
