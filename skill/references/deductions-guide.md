# Chapter VI-A Deductions — Old Regime Complete Guide

## 80C — Most Common Deductions (Max ₹1,50,000 combined)

| Investment / Payment | Notes |
|---|---|
| EPF contribution (employee share) | Auto-included from Form 16 |
| PPF deposits | Passbook / bank statement |
| ELSS mutual funds | Tax-saving MF (3-year lock-in) |
| Life insurance premiums | For self, spouse, children |
| Term insurance premiums | Included in 80C |
| ULIP premiums | Subject to conditions |
| NSC certificates | Accrued interest also qualifies |
| Senior Citizen Savings Scheme | — |
| Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana | For girl child |
| 5-year bank FD (tax-saver) | Lock-in 5 years |
| Home loan principal repayment | Registration + stamp duty in first year |
| Children tuition fees | Full-time education, 2 children max |
| NPS Tier 1 (own contribution) | Also see 80CCD(1B) for extra ₹50k |

## 80CCD — NPS Contributions

| Sub-section | What | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| 80CCD(1) | Employee NPS contribution | Within 80C limit of ₹1.5L |
| 80CCD(1B) | Employee NPS extra | Additional ₹50,000 (OVER and ABOVE 80C) |
| 80CCD(2) | Employer NPS contribution | Up to 14% of salary (allowed in NEW regime too) |

## 80E — Education Loan Interest
- Interest paid on loan for higher education (self, spouse, children, student for whom taxpayer is guardian)
- No limit on amount
- Available for 8 years from start of repayment
- Only interest — not principal

## 80EEA — Home Loan Interest (Affordable Housing)
- Additional ₹1,50,000 over Section 24(b)
- Loan sanctioned between 1 Apr 2019 – 31 Mar 2022
- Stamp duty value of property ≤ ₹45 lakhs
- First-time buyer

## 80G — Donations
| Category | Deduction |
|---|---|
| PM Relief Fund, National Defence Fund, etc | 100% without limit |
| Certain approved institutions | 100% up to 10% of adjusted GTI |
| Other approved institutions | 50% up to 10% of adjusted GTI |

Cash donations > ₹2,000 not eligible. Must have 80G receipt with registration number.

## 80GG — Rent Paid (If No HRA)
- For those NOT receiving HRA from employer
- Deduction = least of: (a) ₹5,000/month, (b) 25% of total income, (c) rent - 10% of income
- Cannot own any residential property in India

## 80TTA — Savings Account Interest (Non-Senior Citizens)
- Up to ₹10,000 deduction on savings bank interest
- Includes interest from banks, post office, co-operative banks

## 80TTB — Interest Income (Senior Citizens Only)
- Up to ₹50,000 for all interest income (savings, FD, RD)
- Supersedes 80TTA for senior citizens

## Section 24(b) — Home Loan Interest
| Property Type | Limit |
|---|---|
| Self-occupied | ₹2,00,000 per year |
| Let-out | No limit (actual interest) |
| Under construction (pre-EMI) | Deferred; 1/5th per year after possession |

## HRA Exemption — Calculation Rules
Exempt = Minimum of:
1. Actual HRA received
2. Actual rent paid minus 10% of (Basic + DA)
3. 50% of Basic+DA (metros) or 40% (non-metros)

Metro cities: Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata

## New Regime — Allowed Deductions Only
- Standard deduction ₹75,000 (Section 16ia)
- Professional tax (Section 16iii)
- Employer NPS contribution (Section 80CCD(2)) — up to 14% of salary
- Interest on let-out property (Section 24) — for let-out only
- DTAA tax relief (Schedule TR)
- TCS credit
- Rebate u/s 87A up to ₹60,000 (if income ≤ ₹12L net of standard deduction)
